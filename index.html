What I do

Lead

I've contributed to mature content teams and worked as a sole UX Writer across multiple teams.

Building from zero

I introduced content design to Backbase. I created frameworks including principles, standards, guidelines, and acceptance criteria that teams actually wanted to use.

Quality metrics

I wrote and socialised quality standards. I made them a reality through Definitions of Done in Jira, Figma widgets, and training sessions that helped teams know what "good" looks like.

Content coherence

With the support of Marketing, I translated vague brand aspirations into three clear principles. These became the foundation for our UX design language. The work gave coherence to diverse teams.

Manage

Good content doesn't happen by accident. It needs processes, tools, and people who know how to use them. Here's how I set those up.

People-led processes

I set up processes according to three pillars: Inspire, Guide and Support. This gave structure to our way of working, and set clear expectations for teams.

The right tools

I led proof of concepts for Lokalise and Figma string variables, working with engineers to find solutions that work for designers, developers and outsourced translators.

Training that sticks

As the first content hire, I hosted office hours and created self-guided training sessions. My UX Writing courses became part of onboarding for designers.

Guide

Guidelines only work if people use them. I create practical guidance that helps teams write well without needing a content designer for every decision.

Content types

I created detailed guidance, cheat sheets, and visual examples for confirmation prompts, errors, notifications, and more. I used varied formats to cater for diverse learning styles.

Frameworks that scale

I used a "Hum, Sing, Shout" framework for success messages. Hums confirm everyday tasks, Sings mark meaningful achievements, Shouts celebrate major milestones. The framework was adopted for by design and interaction teams across design patterns.

Useful style guides

I built copy style guides with both visual and textual guidance, with localised instances. I added Jira Definitions of Done so teams could check their own work. Engineers used the guides to automate checks with linting tools.

Tone parameters

By plotting writing across tone parameters like power, relationship, and expertise, we communicate intention behind tone. This lifts tone from copywriting technique to strategic intent.

Tone variation

These are tone guidelines that dial brand principles up or down based on context, for example, market segment or journey. Both writers and language models used the guidelines.

Brand culture

Although to some degree, we can automate tone with AI, we still need people to love our brands. Tools like brand personality cards add storytelling and build enviable cultures.
